  • Southern Song

    The Southern Song Dynasty (1127 AD-1279 AD) was an ancient Chinese dynasty that inherited the Northern Song Dynasty and the Lower Qi Yuan Dynasty. It was founded by Zhao Gou and settled in Lin'an. It is collectively known as the Song Dynasty with the Northern Song Dynasty.

    Hangzhou Bay Bridge

    Hangzhou Bay Sea Cross Bridge is a cross-sea bridge spanning the Hangzhou Bay in China. It opened to traffic on May 1, 2008.

    West Lake Longjing

    West Lake Longjing belongs to green tea, one of China's top ten famous teas. Produced in the mountains around Longjing Village, West Lake, Hangzhou, Zhejiang Province, hence the name. More than 1200 years old

  • Beijing-Hangzhou Grand Canal

    The Beijing-Hangzhou Grand Canal is a trunk line of the Grand Canal, located in eastern China. It is listed as a World Cultural Heritage and it is the eastern route of the "South-to-North Water Diversion" project. It is also one of the longest, largest and oldest canals in the world.

    Dongpo Meat

    Dongpo meat, a famous dish in Hangzhou, is popular in Jiangsu and Zhejiang. According to legend, it was created by the Northern Song poet Su Dongpo.

    Liangzhu Culture

    The representative site of Liangzhu Culture is the Liangzhu Site, which is about 5300-4000 years ago. The central areas of Liangzhu culture are in the Qiantang River Basin and Taihu Lake Basin

  • West Lake

    West Lake, located at No. 1 Longjing Road, Xihu District, Hangzhou City, Zhejiang Province, west of Hangzhou, has a total area of 49 square kilometers, a catchment area of 21.22 square kilometers, and a lake area of 6.38 square kilometers.

    Thousand Island Lake

    Thousand Island Lake, or Xin 'anjiang Reservoir, is located in Chun 'an County, Hangzhou City, Zhejiang Province. A small part of it is connected to the northwest of jiande city City, Hangzhou City. It is an artificial lake built for the construction of Xin 'anjiang Hydropower Station to impound the lower reaches of Xin 'anjiang River.

    Tang Wei

    Tang Wei [1] (October 7, 1979-), born in Wenzhou, Zhejiang Province, was born in Hangzhou, Zhejiang Province. He graduated from the Central Academy of Drama and an actress in Mainland China.
