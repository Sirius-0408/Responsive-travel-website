Basic introduction

Hangzhou West Lake Scenic Area is located in the center of Hangzhou City, Zhejiang Province. It is divided into lakeside area, lake center area, Beishan area, Nanshan area and Qiantang area. The beautiful lakes and mountains and numerous scenic spots and historic sites are famous both at home and abroad. They are famous tourist resorts in China and are also known as paradise on earth. The mountains in the scenic spot are not more than 400 meters high, and they encircle the south, west and north sides of the West Lake. Among them, Wu Shan and Baoshi Mountain are like two arms, one south and the other north, extending to the urban area, forming a graceful outline of Hang Cheng space. The total area of the scenic spot is 49 square kilometers, of which the lake surface is 6.5 square kilometers, with the lake as the main body. It was formerly called Wulin Water, Qiantang Lake and Xizi Lake. It was called West Lake in Song Dynasty. A large number of trees and shrubs make up a well-organized space with different sizes. Plant landscaping is the main method, supplemented by pavilions, terraces, buildings, pavilions, corridors, pavilions, bridges and pavilions. The West Lake is prosperous near Hangzhou, which is famous for the West Lake. On May 8, 2007, it was officially approved by the National Tourism Administration as a national AAAAA tourist attraction. On June 24, 2011, "West Lake Cultural Landscape in Hangzhou, China" was officially included in the World Heritage List.

Broken bridge

The West Lake Broken Bridge is located at the water dividing point between Hangzhou's Beili Lake and the Outer West Lake, with one end straddling Beishan Road and the other end connected to the white embankment. It is said that the bridge was completed as early as the Tang Dynasty, the Song Dynasty called the Blessing Bridge, and the Yuan Dynasty called the Duan Jia Bridge. The name of the broken bridge came from Tang Dynasty. The origin of its name, saying that the road to Gushan was cut off here, so it was named. It is said that Duanjia Bridge is simply called Duanjia Bridge. Its homophonic name is Duanjia Bridge. There is a stele pavilion in the northeast of the bridge with a "Broken Bridge and Broken Snow" stele inside.

Leifeng Pagoda

Leifeng Pagoda, also known as Huangfei Pagoda and Xiguan Brick Pagoda, is located on Leifeng in Xizhao Mountain on the south bank of West Lake. Leifeng Pagoda was first built in 977 by King Qian Hongchu of Wu Yuezhong and Yi to worship Buddha's Luoji hair relic. The tower was named the "Imperial Concubine Tower" because it came at a time when Sun Shi, wife of the late posthumous posthumous Qian Hongchu of the Northern Song Dynasty, was named the "Imperial Concubine Tower". Later, it was gradually called the "Leifeng Pagoda" because its mountain peak was called "Leifeng".

Su causeway

Su Di is located on the west side of the West Lake, from the foot of Nanping Mountain in the south to the foot of Qixia Ridge in the north. It is nearly 3 kilometers in length. It was built by dredging the West Lake and using excavated fenchyl mud when Su Dongpo, a poet of the Northern Song Dynasty, was appointed to Zhizhou in Hangzhou. Later generations named Su Di in memory of Su Dongpo's harnessing of the West Lake. Six single-hole stone arch bridges were built along the embankment, including Yingbo, Suolan, Wangshan, Yadi, Dongpu and Trans-Hong.

Geographical environment

Position

Hangzhou West Lake Scenic Area is located in the west of Hangzhou City. It is located in the area where plains, hills, lakes and rivers meet. It is surrounded by mountains on three sides and borders the city on one side. It is divided into lakeside area, lake center area, Beishan area, Nanshan area and Qiantang area, with a total area of 49 square kilometers, including 6.5 square kilometers of lake surface. The West Lake is prosperous near Hangzhou, which is famous for the West Lake.

Climate

It is a subtropical monsoon climate with warm and humid climate and four distinct seasons. The annual average temperature is 16.2℃, the summer average temperature is 28.6℃, the winter average temperature is 3.8℃, and the frost-free period is 230-260 days. The annual average rainfall is 1500 mm, and the average relative humidity is relatively high, between 70% and 85%. With the reverse conversion of winter and summer monsoon and other weather conditions, obvious seasonal changes will take place, forming the climatic characteristics of rainy spring, humid summer, cool autumn and dry and cold winter. Hangzhou is connected with the sea in the east and the mountains in the west. The topography is complex and varied. The geographical distribution of light, heat and water is uneven. The microclimate resources are abundant. However, the instability of monsoon in advancing and retreating duration and intensity often leads to meteorological disasters.

Hydrological

West Lake is a semi-closed still water lake with green water in it. The lakes and mountains complement each other. It is a polygon close to the same axis. The lake is separated by Gushan, Baidi, Suti and Yang Gongdi. According to the size of the area, it is divided into five water surfaces, namely, Outer West Lake, Xili Lake (also known as "Rear West Lake" or "Rear Lake"), Beili Lake (also known as "Inner West Lake"), Xiaohu Lake (also known as "South Lake") and Yuehu Lake, of which Outer West Lake has the largest area. Gushan is the largest natural island in the west lake. the su causeway and the Bai causeway cross the lake. three artificial islets, Xiaoying chau, huxin pavilion and ruangong dun, stand in the center of the outer west lake. leifeng pagoda in xizhao mountain and baochu pagoda in Baoshi mountain form a basic pattern of "one mountain, two towers, three islands, three causeways and five lakes" across the lake. The sections of the lake are connected by bridges. The water area of the West Lake includes 6.5 square kilometers of islands in the lake and 15 kilometers of the perimeter of the lake bank. The deepest part is about 5 meters, the shallowest part is less than 1 meter, and the average water depth is 2.27 meters. The water volume is about 14.29 million cubic meters. The north of Hunan is 3.3km long and 2.8km wide from east to west.

Since 2002, Hangzhou has started to implement the comprehensive protection project of the West Lake, which has carried out all-round protection and renovation of the West Lake's ecological protection, environmental optimization, continuation of cultural context and construction of a new countryside. The "New West Lake" has been launched eight times in a row. Through the West Lake dredging project and the introduction of Qiantang River water into the lake, the water depth of the West Lake has increased from 1.65 meters to 2.5 meters, and the transparency of the water body has increased from 50 centimeters to 73 centimeters.

Historical evolution

Origin of name

The West Lake was originally a bay. It evolved from a bay to a lagoon, and from the lagoon to an ordinary lake. The West Lake was originally a natural lake and was flooded with waste during its development. West Lake is a lake in Qiantang County, so it is called Qiantang Lake (from Tang Dynasty onwards, "Qiantang" was changed to "Qiantang"), and because there is also a lake connected to it in the north, the water level is lower than it, so it is called Xiahu, so the West Lake is also called Shanghu accordingly. The acquisition of the name West Lake is the result of the historical development and geographical changes in Qiantang County. Since Qin Dynasty set up Qiantang County in the mountains of West Lake, the county government may have moved away from the mountains and gradually entered the plain through the Han, the Three Kingdoms, the two Jin and the Southern and Northern Dynasties. In the 11th year of Emperor Wen's reign, Qiantang County moved to Liupu in the foothills of Phoenix and was promoted to the state administration of Hangzhou.

In the second year of Changqing (822), in order to increase the water storage capacity of the West Lake, Bai Juyi built a lake bank near Shihan Bridge, that is, around the Children's Palace, several feet higher than the original lake bank. This was originally the connection between the upper lake and the lower source. The water level of the West Lake was higher than that of the lower lake. Bai Juyi's embankment created a greater gap between the water level of the upper and lower lakes. Although the lower lake has been flooded for a long time, this artificial terrain gap still exists obviously in the two directions of the hydropower station at least in the north of the west lake bank near the children's palace and the west ring road in the east. Bai Juyi's construction of the lake embankment at the beginning of the ninth century was epoch-making for the development of the West Lake, because since then the nature of the West Lake has changed and it has evolved from a natural lake to a man-made lake. The West Lake changed its nature due to the embankment built by Bai Juyi, who still calls it Qiantang Lake. When the name West Lake became popular, West Lake was already an artificial lake.

After the West Lake became an artificial lake, the West Lake was renovated in the Wu Yue State era. After the Northern Song Dynasty, Wang Ji of Zhizhou in the 4th year of Jingde (1007) not only dredged the whole lake, but also built the sluice and weir equipment of the West Lake. Zheng Jian of Zhizhou and Shen Lu of Song Renshou era (1023-1063), the former used tens of thousands of migrant workers, denounced fenchyl fields in the waste lake and carried out large-scale dredging. In addition to the six wells, the latter set up a new well with extremely large water supply, which is called Shen Gong well by later generations. Su Shi carefully studied the West Lake Water Conservancy in 1069 in the second year of Yu Xi Ning's reign and explored the plan to unblock six wells and Shen Gong's well. In the first year of Yuan You (1086), Su Shi came to Hangzhou for the second time to take up the post of Zhizhou, worked out a plan for dredging the West Lake, and presented the throne of "Begging to Open the West Lake in Hangzhou" to the imperial court. The paper is the first time that the name "West Lake" is used in official documents. It shows that Hangzhou city has been built to the east of West Lake and the name of West Lake has become popular.

CultureDevelopment record

Old Ten Scenes

Originated from the title of West Lake Landscape Painting in Southern Song Dynasty. Each of the ten scenic spots is good at winning. The common point is that the scenic spots are all near or in the lake. When the Song Dynasty died in the Yuan Dynasty, the ten scenic spots of the West Lake were once neglected and depressed, and the scenic spots referred to in the scenic spots were hard to find. In the Ming Dynasty, the Ten Scenes were restored and made achievements. In the 38th year of the reign of Emperor Kangxi of the Qing Dynasty (1699), Emperor Kangxi made a southern tour to Hangzhou, marking the ten scenic spots of the West Lake one by one, changing "two peaks inserted into clouds" to "two peaks inserted into clouds"; "Thunder Peak Falling Light" (or "Thunder Peak Evening Light") is changed to "Thunder Peak West Light"; "Nanping Night Clock" is changed to "Nanping Morning Clock". Although "Xizhao" and "Xiaozhong" have only been changed in one word, they have not been accepted by the public. Therefore, only in Qing Dynasty's works on West Lake do they have changed their names. Later, they wrote poems, lyrics and articles, and still use their initial names. After Emperor Kangxi inscribed the Ten Scenes, Zhejiang local officials successively inscribed the names of the scenes written by Yubi, carved stones, erected steles, and built pavilions to protect them. Thus, the names of the Ten Scenes have been recorded from only the books in the past and become the signs of the scenic spots where the Ten Scenes are located. Later, Emperor Ganlong made a southern tour of Hangzhou and wrote a poem on each of the ten scenes, which was inscribed on the shady side of the stele of the king tablet, making the names of the ten scenes of the West Lake more widely known. In addition, many paintings, poems, travel notes and photos depicting the ten scenes in Song, Yuan, Ming, Qing and modern times were recognized as the representatives of the landscape of the West Lake. The ten sights of West Lake except "Leifeng Evening Sun" collapsed in 13 years (1924), and the landscape disappeared. The remaining nine scenes were renovated, restored and expanded. Not only did they take on a completely new look, but their contents also matched the name of the scene.
