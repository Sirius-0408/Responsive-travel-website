    Battle of Yiling

    The Battle of Yiling (also known as the Battle of Yiling and the Battle of Xiāotíng (now north of Yidu, Hubei)) was a large-scale battle that took place during the Three Kingdoms period and was waged by Shu Bei Zhaolie Emperor Liu Bei against Eastern Wu. It is a successful example of the famous active defense in the history of ancient wars in China, and the last of the "three major battles" of the Three Kingdoms.

    Yangtze Three Gorges

    The Three Gorges of the Yangtze River [1] is the general name of the three sections of the canyons of Qutang Gorge, Wu Gorge, and Xiling Gorge. It is located on the mainstream of the Yangtze River in Chongqing and Hubei Province, China, from Baidi City in Fengjie County, Chongqing City, to Yichang City, Hubei Province in the east Nanjin Pass, with a total length of 193 kilometers.
