Basic introduction

The Beijing Palace Museum was established on October 10, 1925 and is located in the Forbidden City of Beijing Palace Museum. It is a comprehensive museum in China established on the basis of the palaces of the Ming and Qing dynasties and their collections. It is also the largest ancient cultural and art museum in China. Its collection of cultural relics mainly comes from the old collections in the palaces of the Qing dynasty and is the first batch of national patriotism education demonstration bases. Since January 1, 2014, the Palace Museum in Beijing has closed almost every Monday. The Beijing Palace Museum is located in Beijing's Forbidden City. The Forbidden City in Beijing was the first batch of national key cultural relics protection units, the first batch of national 5A-level tourist attractions, and the first batch of national advanced units for ideological and moral construction of minors. It was included in the World Cultural Heritage List in 1987. In October 2018, the Palace Museum released the first thematic functional games and the first album of ancient paintings themed music, which kicked off the "Wisdom Palace".

Scenic spot landscape

Hall of Supreme Harmony

The hall of supreme harmony is the supreme golden palace in the forbidden city, the so-called "golden palace" in the imperial palace. this is the place where the emperor held important ceremonies. It was first built in the Yongle Year of the Ming Dynasty (early 15th century) and was destroyed and rebuilt by several disasters. Ming jiajing dynasty changed its name to imperial palace. After the Manchu Dynasty established its capital Beijing, it changed its name to the present, which contains the grand purport of world harmony. The hall of supreme harmony was rebuilt and preserved in the Kangxi year of Qing dynasty.

Hall of Central Harmony

The Hall of Zhonghe was built in the 18th year of Ming Yongle (1420 A.D.). It went through three fires and was rebuilt in the 7th year of tomorrow (1627 A.D.). It was first named Huagai Hall and later changed to Zhongji Hall. It was called Zhonghe Hall only in the 2nd year of Qing Shunzhi (1645 A.D.). The top of Baohe Hall is a double-eaves, two-part mountain style. The hall follows the French style of "building by reducing pillars" of the Song and Yuan Dynasties, with open space. In the Qing Dynasty, it was a place for banquets and palace examination.

Baohe Palace

Baohe Hall was built in 1420, the 18th year of Yongle in the Ming Dynasty. Its initial name was Jinshen Hall. During the Jiajing period of the Ming Dynasty, there was a fire. After reconstruction, it was renamed Jianji Hall. The second year of Qing Shunzhi (1645) was changed to Baohe Hall, which means "keeping one's mind at rest and keeping one's mind at rest" in order to maintain harmony in the universe, so that one can live happily and enjoy peace in the world. The plaque of Baohe Hall, "The Emperor Built Extremes," is a dry dragon royal pen. Canon: ji zi's Hong Fan "The Emperor Built His Extremes". Couplets hung in Baohe Hall: "Ancestral discipline is clear, and my heir and son Shang Kachin will live forever. The heart of heaven will give warning, but when the ministers of all nations think about it, there will be no end to it. "

jinshui bridge

Jinshui Bridge is located in Taihe Gate Square, with a total of 5 buildings and a single arch. The southern part of the square meanders from west to east along an arched Jinshui River, which is called the "Inner Jinshui Bridge". This is the largest, most spectacular and most colorful group of stone bridges in the Forbidden City. In the middle is the royal road bridge, dedicated to the emperor. On the east and west sides are royal bridges for members of the royal family and ministers. On both sides are grade bridges for officials above grade 3 to walk. The function is the same as that of the Outer Jinshui Bridge in front of Tiananmen Square.

palace of earthly tranquility

The palace of earthly tranquility was built in the 18th year of Ming Yongle (1420 ad). In the Ming Dynasty, it belonged to the queen's bedroom. In the 12th year of Qing Shunzhi (1655 A.D.), it was rebuilt following the style of Qing Ning Palace in Shengjing. Four rooms in the western end were set aside as places for shamanism to worship gods, while two rooms in the eastern end were the bridal chamber for the emperor's wedding. Kangxi, Tongzhi and Guangxu, the three young emperors who ascended the throne, were all married here.

Watch Museum

Permanent exhibition hall. Located in Fengxian Temple District, south of the 6th Palace District in the east of Neiting and west of Ningshou Palace District, it displays Qing Dynasty clocks and watches made in Britain, France, Switzerland and Qing Dynasty construction offices, Suzhou and Guangzhou. The famous bronze and gold-plated writing clocks and watches are displayed here. Documentary films about clocks and watches in the Qing dynasty palace are shown in circulation in the museum. clocks and watches are demonstrated at 11 o'clock and 14 o'clock in the museum. To visit the clock museum, you need to purchase additional tickets for the clock museum (i.e. Fengxian Temple District) in 10 yuan. The ticket office is located outside Chengsu Gate on the south side of Fengxian Temple District.

Ningshou Palace District

Ningshou Palace District is located in the northeast of Miyagi City. It was built during the Ganlong period. It includes Jiulong Wall, Huangji Palace, Ganlong Garden, Zhenfei Well and other famous scenic spots. Treasure Hall, Stone Drum Hall and Opera Hall are three permanent exhibition halls located in Ningshou Palace. Visitors to the scenic spots and exhibition halls in Ningshou Palace District need to purchase additional tickets for the 10 yuan Museum of Treasures (namely, Ningshou Palace District, including the Stone Drum Museum and the Opera Museum). The ticket office is located outside Xiqing Gate on the south side of Ningshou Palace District.

Open time

The Palace Museum is open all year round. The opening hours will be changed according to the light and busy seasons. In case of legal holidays, major events or special circumstances, the opening hours will be extended or shortened. Please pay attention to the announcement on the official website of the Palace Museum at any time.

The Palace Museum announced on November 18, 2013 that it plans to officially close the museum all day on Monday from January 1, 2014, except for legal holidays and summer holidays (from July 1 to August 31 each year). Every year from April 1 to October 31, the peak season opening time will be adopted: ticket sales will start and the opening time for admission will be 8: 30. Tickets will be closed at 16: 00 (including clock museum and treasure museum); The closing time is 16: 10; Cleared at 17:00.

The off-season opening hours will be adopted from November 1 to March 31 of the following year: ticketing will start and admission time will be 8: 30. Tickets will be closed at 15: 30 (including clock museum and treasure museum); Closing time is 15:40.
