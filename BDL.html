Historical evolution

The Ming Dynasty's "Changan Hakka Dialect" said, "The roads are divided from now on and extend in all directions, hence the name Badaling, which is the highest mountain in Guanshan".

The name Badaling was first found in the long poems of the Jin Dynasty poet Liu Ying: "Arrive at Badaling Late, Da Dan Nai Shang" and "Exit Badaling". In Yuan Dynasty, it was called "Beikou" here, which is relative to Nankou. Nankou is located in Changping County, the northern suburb of Beijing. From Nankou to Beikou, there is a 40-mile long canyon in the middle

There is the famous gateway of the great wall, juyongguan, which is why the canyon is called guangou. Badaling stands at the highest point at the northern end of Guangou. Here, the two peaks stand side by side, opening at the same time, commanding, the situation is extremely dangerous. From Badaling down, one looks at Juyongguan, like building a strategic position, like looking into a well. "The ancients had the risk of living in mediocrity, not in Guancheng, but in Badaling." The special terrain of Badaling mountain pass has become a battleground for military strategists of all ages and has always been heavily guarded in Israel. Badaling has become a military strategic location, dating back to the Spring and Autumn and Warring States periods.

According to Records of the Historian and the general survey of cultural relic workers, Badaling area has been proved to have built a Great Wall during the Warring States Period, and remains of remnant walls and piers are still found today. Its trend is generally the same as that of the Great Wall of the Ming Dynasty. According to Han tradition, there were two closed cities of Jundu and Juyong. The northern Wei dynasty's "water sutra notes" said: "juyongguan is located in the region of juyong, hence the name of juyongguan. in the south, juyongguan is located in the valley of juyongguan. the stone barrier is the site of the pass. it is not a light achievement. its water passes through the south of the mountain, and passes through the capital of Jing army …." therefore, some experts believe that the Han dynasty set juyongguan in Badaling. Where did the name "Ju Yong" come from? It was Qin Shihuang who built the Great Wall and migrated a group of poor peasants and prisoners from the south to settle in the small basin of Guichuan in Jundu Mountain, which is called the residence of the Yongtu. During the reign of Emperor Wu of the Han Dynasty, Gui Chuan was placed in Yong County (imitating Yanqing). The topography and landscape of the closed site and the flow of mountains and waters recorded in Shuijingzhu are the same as the geographical environment of Badaling.

The Northern Wei Dynasty built the Great Wall around Badaling 1,500 years ago. According to records in the book of Wei, the emperor Taiping of the northern Wei dynasty built the great wall north of the capital city of Pingcheng (now Datong) in the 7th year (446), called "Ji Shang sai wai", starting from the mountain of shanggu jun du (i.e. Badaling area) in the east and reaching the yellow river bank in the west. Later, he came to Tianbao of the Northern Qi Dynasty for six years (555). He also built the Great Wall, starting from Datong in the west and extending the Great Wall to the seaside via Shandong, the military capital.

Badaling Great Wall was rebuilt in Ming Dynasty. Zhu Yuanzhang, the first emperor of the Ming Dynasty, knew the importance of city walls in military affairs. When he became emperor, he "built high walls" to fortify and prepare for war. At that time, all the state capitals and counties also built cities one after another. Zhu Yuanzhang sent general Xu Da, Feng Sheng and other army troops to build a blockade in the north, build the Great Wall, and step up military training and wasteland in order to prevent the remnants of the Yuan Dynasty from invading south.

In 1403, Zhu Di, the third emperor of the Ming Dynasty, acceded to the throne.

In 1420, he moved his capital from Nanjing to Beijing. Zhu Di was determined to unify China and made five personal expeditions. His army went deep into the Mongolian Plateau as far as the Enen River in Russia. Only after 1488 did the massive and all-round construction of the Great Wall begin. After 270 years, a total of 18 large-scale construction projects have finally completed a high wall, starting from the Yalu River in Liaoning Province in the east and ending in Bulongji west of Jiayuguan in Gansu Province in the west. This is what we usually call the Great Wall. The Ming Great Wall is more than 14600 li long and passes through seven provinces, cities and autonomous regions in northern China. No matter from the level of engineering technology and the degree of fortification, it is incomparable to the previous Great Wall. In this sense, it can be said that the Great Wall we saw was built in the Spring and Autumn Period and the Warring States Period, linked to the Qin Shihuang period and perfected in the Ming Dynasty.

The Great Wall of the Ming Dynasty was no longer a single high wall, but a defense-in-depth system of "layers of defense". Several city walls and passes were also built in key areas. Ningxia, Shaanxi, Shanxi, Hebei and other places have all built double lines of the Great Wall, and Shanxi has set up as many as four. In important traffic and military sites, several closed cities and several shorter walls are built, such as the Shili valley in yanmenguan, with 28 stone walls. it can be said that "every inch of land is blocked and every step is taken".

Badaling is witness to many important events in history.

After the first emperor Qin Shihuang came to Jieshi in the east, he drove back to Xianyang from Badaling via Datong, while Queen Xiao's tour, Yuan Taizu's entry into the customs, the Yuan Dynasty emperor's twice-yearly round trip between Beijing and Shangdu, the Ming Dynasty emperor's northern expedition, and the Qing Dynasty emperor's personal expedition ... Badaling were all necessary places. The number of poems and essays of Badaling chanted by royalty, nobles and literati in past dynasties is even more numerous. Gao Shi, a poet of Tang dynasty, was the first to leave a poem. in his poem, he wrote, "the mountains and clouds are all high when the water and the slope are connected." As for those years of ethnic strife and war, the Badaling Mountains have staged magnificent historical dramas. Now, the sufferings of old China, the 2,000-year history of competing for hegemony among feudal lords and the change of dynasties have all become a distant past. The Great Wall is 100,000 miles long and has a culture of 5,000 years. Times have given Badaling Great Wall a new mission. It is like a precious monument in history, standing on the top of thousands of mountains, shining with the brilliance of the Chinese civilization and wisdom, and showing the great quality and spirit of the Chinese nation's perseverance and perseverance. It has become a bond of friendship linking people of all ethnic groups in our country, overseas Chinese and international friends. It is a precious cultural heritage of mankind.

City wall

The wall of Badaling Great Wall is 6 ~ 9 meters high, with a trapezoidal plane, 6.5 ~ 7.5 meters wide at the bottom and 4.5 ~ 5.8 meters wide at the top. Most of the walls are broad and flat with "five horses riding in parallel and ten people riding in parallel". The Qinglong Bridge area has a steep slope with a narrow wall top, the narrowest point being only more than 2 meters. The center line of the city wall is on the outside, the outer wall is high and the inner wall is low. Both sides of the wall are covered with granite stone strips, which are 0.5 meters wide, 0.4 meters high and 0.8-1 meter long. The longest feldspar strip is 3.1 meters and weighs 1.5-1.7 tons. No matter the steep hillside or the gentle section, the stone strips are laid horizontally layer by layer, crisscrossed, laid horizontally and vertically, biting into one body, and mortar is poured into the joint. Fill the wall with mud and stones, and tamp them down. The wall body consists of 3-4 layers of city bricks, which are glued and sealed with lime putty. The city is paved with wall bricks to make the top of the wall flat. Wall bricks are 0.4 meters long, 0.2 meters wide and 0.1 meters thick. Each brick weighs 15kg. In steep sections with large slope, ladders are built to facilitate up and down. There are brick parapets on both sides of the top of the wall. The outer side is called crenelation or battlements. The inner side is called parapet or parapet. The battlements are facing the enemy and are 1.7 ~ 2 meters high, which is convenient to cover the human body. Generally, the battlements are built at the chest height of the human body, and the distance between the battlements is about 1 meter. The brick at the mouth of the pile is specially made, with one side of the pile being bevelled, and the pile is stacked on the mouth in a fan-shaped shape, with a wide outside and a narrow inside, and a large observation surface. An observation hole is arranged at the upper part of the retaining pile. Perforations are built at the lower part of the wall to shoot arrows and throw stones. The brick faces of some perforation and observation holes are carved with patterns. The inner wall is about 1m high. The battlements and walls are made of city bricks, and the top of the wall is made of a layer of ridge bricks. Both sides of the ridge brick are low and the middle is high, or the inner side is high and the outer side is low, which is convenient for drainage. Roadways with walls are formed at the top of the city wall, and small water tanks are built at the wall roots on both sides. The accumulated water flows along the water tanks to the lower part of the Yu wall at the lower part. The water is led to the water spouts outside the wall through the water outlet holes and then flows out of the city wall. The spout is a stone trough more than 1 meter long, inside the Great Wall, extending beyond the wall. At regular intervals, there is an entrance to the city wall. The entrance is a ticket gate, 1.8m high and 0.8m wide. The door frame is made of 4 pieces of stone with the same specifications. There are stone steps in the door leading to the top of the city wall. The city walls are built with brick water gates across streams and shahe rivers. some are as big as city gates, such as the shifosi water gate. Battle station

Built along the Great Wall, the main traffic arteries or the dangerous terrain are blockhouses with one, two or three floors and varying sizes.
